Journal of Atmospheric and Oceanic Technology (AMS)

Impacts of Training Data Properties on U-Net Based Ocean Mixed Layer Depth Inversion in Northwest Pacific

This is the clearest same-week satellite-centered paper in the approved source list.

  • The system inverts mixed-layer depth from satellite-observed sea-surface temperature, sea-surface height, and wind fields.
  • It reports useful skill in the Northwest Pacific and reduced errors during post-tropical-cyclone mixed-layer deepening.
  • The paper is especially relevant because it turns surface remote-sensing information into a dynamically meaningful upper-ocean product.

Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society (AMS)

Lowering Barriers to the Adoption and Mainstreaming of Earth Observation Data: The Global Precipitation Measurement Mission (GPM) Mentorship Program

This item fits the satellite window from the user-adoption side rather than the retrieval-algorithm side.

  • Centers on satellite precipitation data from the GPM mission and the barriers that often keep Earth-observation data from broader use.
  • Describes a mentorship model mixing lectures, demonstrations, and a capstone workflow for an international participant base.
  • Useful for readers tracking how satellite products move from expert-only tools toward wider operational and societal use.
