Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Characteristics of Layers of Enhanced Spectrum Width within Northeast United States Winter Precipitation Events

A radar-based climatology of enhanced Doppler spectrum width layers gives forecasters a clearer picture of shear and turbulence structures inside winter precipitation systems.

  • Uses Ka-band scanning polarimetric radar observations from Long Island to document enhanced spectrum-width layers in winter storms.
  • Accessible abstract text says the study provides a first climatology of these layers from 2017 to 2020.
  • It is relevant to radar interpretation of internal storm structure during cold-season precipitation events.

Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Meteorological Benefits and Operational Applications of S-Band, Rapid-Scanning, Dual-Polarization Radar for Hail-Producing Storms

Rapid-scan dual-polarization radar speaks directly to warning operations for fast-evolving hailstorms.

  • The abstract states that traditional radar update cycles can miss rapidly evolving hailstorm processes.
  • This study evaluates the operational benefits of fast S-band dual-polarization scanning.
  • It is a strong radar-operations paper for severe convective monitoring.

Journal of Atmospheric and Oceanic Technology (AMS)

Improving NEXRAD Velocity Retrievals Using Multi-PRT Scans with Regression Processing

Velocity ambiguity is a classic operational radar problem, so improvements here have immediate value for weather surveillance.

  • The abstract highlights range–velocity ambiguity as a key pulsed-Doppler radar data-quality issue.
  • The paper proposes multi-PRT scans with regression processing to improve unambiguous velocity retrieval.
  • This is directly relevant to long-range radar surveillance and severe-weather diagnosis.
