Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Atlantic tropical easterly wave and cyclogenesis forecasting in the physical and new AI forecast systems at ECMWF

  • Compares ECMWF's physical Integrated Forecasting System and its newer AI Forecasting System for African easterly waves and Atlantic cyclogenesis.
  • Shows strong case-to-case variability, with probabilities often jumping between 72 and 48 hours as systems become better organized.
  • The AI-based ensemble often scored higher than the physical ensemble at 84–120 hour lead times, especially for stronger waves.

Evaluating the Performance of AI-based and Traditional NWP Guidance for High Impact Winter Storms over the U.S. East Coast

  • Tests whether AI-NWP guidance remains reliable during high-impact winter storms rather than only on average benchmark cases.
  • Compares several leading AI systems against traditional numerical guidance using precipitation and storm-structure metrics.
  • The abstract reports regional RMSE improvements on the order of 20–30% for some variables, while still emphasizing the need for event-based scrutiny.

Warning Value Chain Assessments for Storms Eunice (2022) and Ciarán (2023): Insights and Recommendations

  • Not a core NWP-method paper, but useful for connecting forecast production to warning delivery and downstream decision support.
  • Compares two damaging storms with different hazard footprints and examines how information moved through the warning chain.

Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

A Sensor-Driven Wind-Farm Parameterization in WRF using a Skewed Gaussian Wind-Turbine Wake Model

  • Addresses a known limitation of coarse-resolution NWP models when representing turbine-to-turbine wake effects inside wind farms.
  • Introduces a new WRF parameterization that samples undisturbed inflow with virtual sensors to reduce double-counting of wake effects.
  • Relevant as an example of model-physics refinement inside an operationally important forecasting framework.

Journal of Atmospheric and Oceanic Technology (AMS)

Impacts of Training Data Properties on U-Net Based Ocean Mixed Layer Depth Inversion in Northwest Pacific

  • Builds a U-Net inversion system for daily mixed-layer depth using satellite-observed sea-surface temperature, sea-surface height, and wind fields.
  • Reports useful skill in the Northwest Pacific, including improved depiction of mixed-layer deepening after tropical cyclones.
  • Fits the satellite window because it turns satellite-observed surface variables into an ocean structure product with forecasting relevance.

Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society (AMS)

Lowering Barriers to the Adoption and Mainstreaming of Earth Observation Data: The Global Precipitation Measurement Mission (GPM) Mentorship Program

  • Focuses on how satellite precipitation data from the GPM mission can be made more usable for research and societal applications.
  • Summarizes a mentorship model that combined lectures, hands-on exercises, and capstone work for participants across 71 countries.
  • Included because it shows the practical side of satellite-data adoption, not just algorithm development.
