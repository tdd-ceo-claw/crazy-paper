npj Climate and Atmospheric Science (Nature)

Primary: Radar

A nationwide radar network reveals typhoon wind profile characteristics in the lower troposphere over China

Publication date: 2026-04-14

  • Abstract snippet says the study analyzes 186 wind-profile radars during 45 typhoons affecting China from 2020 to 2024, allowing systematic characterization of typhoon wind profiles up to 2 km.
  • The summary indicates four recurring lower-tropospheric profile types, with structure varying by storm quadrant, distance from the core, terrain, and typhoon intensity.
  • This is the cleanest same-week radar-centered paper in the approved-source pool, so it anchors the radar bucket and remains on the all page as the strongest observational lead.

Atmosphere (MDPI)

Primary: NWPSecondary: AI-assisted forecasting

A Tropical Depression over the South China Sea in June 2025—Observational and Forecasting Aspects

Publication date: 2026-04-14

  • The abstract documents a June 2025 South China Sea tropical depression and focuses on operational forecasting questions including upgrade timing, center placement under a multiple-center situation, intensity, and Hong Kong wind impact.
  • It explicitly states that artificial-intelligence models gave earlier alerting for genesis and coastal motion, while conventional physics-based models remained useful for forecasting the local wind impact.
  • Because the paper is fundamentally an operations-and-forecasting case study with AI as one component, it is classified primarily as NWP and visibly labeled as a secondary AI item.

Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Primary: NWPSecondary: AI

Atlantic tropical easterly wave and cyclogenesis forecasting in the physical and new AI forecast systems at ECMWF

Publication date: same-window metadata / abstract-snippet level only

  • Accessible metadata indicates a head-to-head comparison between ECMWF's physical Integrated Forecasting System and its newer AI Forecasting System for tropical easterly waves and cyclogenesis prediction.
  • The usable abstract-level description supports a cautious takeaway: case dependence remains substantial, but the AI ensemble appears stronger at longer lead times for stronger wave cases.
  • This stays in the NWP bucket because forecast-system verification is the professional center of gravity, while the secondary label keeps the AI dimension visible.
