Recent work on radar data assimilation, satellite-informed observing systems, and operational / subseasonal numerical forecasting drawn from the approved journal sources.

Collection for this week relied on accessible abstracts and metadata from the approved journal list. Some source endpoints rate-limited direct retrieval, so selections were kept to papers whose abstract-level relevance was still visible.

Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Radar Data Assimilation with JEDI LETKF for Ensemble Forecasting of Hurricane Ida (2021) Using a HAFS-Like Configuration

Why it matters: Radar assimilation for landfalling hurricanes is exactly the sort of high-impact bridge between observations and short-range NWP that this bundle is meant to track.

  • The abstract says the study tests direct radar assimilation inside the JEDI Local Ensemble Transform Kalman Filter framework.
  • Experiments use a HAFS-like configuration for short-range ensemble forecasts of Hurricane Ida.
  • It is a strong crossover paper for both radar meteorology and hurricane NWP.

Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Ensemble Forecast Sensitivity to Observations (EFSO) Applied to All-sky Infrared Radiance Observations

Why it matters: This paper asks how much all-sky infrared radiances matter to forecast improvement, making it an observing-system study with direct satellite relevance.

  • The abstract frames EFSO as a way to estimate each observation’s contribution to forecast improvement.
  • Here the method is applied specifically to all-sky infrared radiance observations.
  • That makes the paper highly relevant to satellite data usage and observing-system design for NWP.

Journal of Hydrometeorology (AMS)

Evaluation of Radar-Derived Polarimetric Precipitation Estimates for Extreme Rain Events Using a Dense Network of Rain Gauges

Why it matters: Quantitative precipitation estimation is where radar performance meets hydrologic consequences, especially during extreme rain.

  • The study evaluates radar-based polarimetric precipitation estimates for ten extreme rain events in the Kansas City area.
  • Accessible abstract text notes hourly and roughly 0.5 km scale comparisons using a dense rain-gauge network.
  • It is a practical radar-QPE assessment paper with direct relevance to flood monitoring and verification.

Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (AGU)

Long-Range Transport Pathways of Intense Wildfire Plumes in the Northern Hemisphere Observed by the IASI/METOP Satellite Mission

Why it matters: Satellite missions are often the only practical way to follow hazardous plume transport across basins and continents.

  • The abstract presents a back-trajectory analysis built from satellite observations of carbon monoxide and peroxyacetyl nitrate.
  • It focuses on long-range transport of extreme wildfire plumes across the Northern Hemisphere.
  • Although not a weather-forecast paper, it is a clear satellite-observing-system study relevant to atmospheric monitoring.

Bulletin of the American Meteorological Society (AMS)

The Operational Forecast Process at MetCoOp

Why it matters: This paper gives a rare operations-focused look at how multiple national services jointly produce regional NWP guidance.

  • The abstract describes a cooperative regional forecast-production system maintained by several Nordic and Baltic meteorological services.
  • Its emphasis is operational NWP workflow, not just model theory.
  • That makes it valuable context for readers interested in how forecast guidance is actually produced and shared.

Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (AGU)

Subseasonal Forecasting of Snow Cover and Cold Compound Extremes: Insights From MPAS-A Over Midlatitude East Asia

Why it matters: Subseasonal prediction is still a frontier problem, and this paper makes the challenge concrete through snow–atmosphere interactions and compound cold hazards.

  • The abstract evaluates subseasonal forecast skill for snow cover and cold compound extremes in East Asia.
  • It uses MPAS-A and highlights snow–atmosphere interactions as an extra source of forecast difficulty.
  • This is a clean numerical-weather-prediction article at longer lead times.
