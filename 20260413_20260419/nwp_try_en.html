Atmosphere (MDPI)

A Tropical Depression over the South China Sea in June 2025—Observational and Forecasting Aspects

Publication date: 2026-04-14

  • Documents a June 2025 South China Sea tropical depression and focuses on operational questions such as upgrade timing, center location in a multiple-center situation, intensity, and Hong Kong wind impacts.
  • The abstract reports that AI models provided earlier alerts for genesis and coastal motion, while conventional physics-based models remained useful for forecasting local wind impacts.
  • Also includes wind-structure analysis using dropsonde and wind-profiler data, making it a solid same-week operations-and-forecasting addition from the newly added MDPI source.

Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Atlantic tropical easterly wave and cyclogenesis forecasting in the physical and new AI forecast systems at ECMWF

Publication date: same-window metadata only

  • Compares ECMWF's physical Integrated Forecasting System with its newer AI Forecasting System for African easterly waves and Atlantic cyclogenesis cases.
  • The abstract-level summary indicates strong case-to-case variability, but better AI-ensemble skill than the physical ensemble at longer lead times for stronger waves.
  • Kept in the rebuilt bundle because it remains one of the clearest forecasting-method papers associated with this issue window from the approved AMS list.

Evaluating the Performance of AI-based and Traditional NWP Guidance for High Impact Winter Storms over the U.S. East Coast

Publication date: same-window metadata only

  • Examines whether AI-based guidance remains reliable during high-impact East Coast winter storms rather than only on average benchmark cases.
  • The accessible abstract-level snippet emphasizes comparisons with traditional numerical guidance using storm-structure and precipitation metrics, with notable error reductions for some variables.
  • Included as a second NWP-method paper, but described conservatively because the accessible evidence is snippet-level rather than full abstract text.
