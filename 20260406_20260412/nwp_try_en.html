Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Examining the Impact of Different Uncrewed Aerial System (UAS) Sampling Strategies on Fog Forecasts in the Ohio Valley Using Observing System Simulation Experiments (OSSEs)

Primary: Numerical Weather Prediction

Why it matters: A genuinely forecast-oriented same-week Weather and Forecasting paper rather than a loose operations note.

  • The abstract uses OSSEs for three impactful Ohio Valley fog events to compare two UAS sampling strategies.
  • Both strategies improve fog forecast skill after assimilation, showing that denser nocturnal boundary-layer observations can matter.
  • A profiling-style UAS network improves temperature and dewpoint analyses aloft more strongly, which the authors tie to broader aviation-hazard forecasting value.

Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (AGU)

Subseasonal Forecasting of Snow Cover and Cold Compound Extremes: Insights From MPAS-A Over Midlatitude East Asia

Primary: Numerical Weather Prediction

Why it matters: A strong NWP pick because the abstract is explicit about lead-time skill, error sources, and model-physics sensitivity.

  • The abstract says MPAS-A can forecast rapid snow-cover expansion and temperature drops up to two pentads ahead, with detectable skill at three pentads.
  • Beyond four pentads the forecasts become unreliable, giving a clear upper bound on useful subseasonal skill in this setup.
  • Biases come mainly from underestimated snowfall, and the results are sensitive to the land-surface snow-cover-fraction scheme.

Atmosphere (MDPI)

A Tropical Depression over the South China Sea in June 2025—Observational and Forecasting Aspects

Primary: Numerical Weather PredictionSecondary: AI

Why it matters: Useful because the abstract explicitly compares earlier AI alerts with conventional model value in local wind-impact forecasting.

  • The accessible abstract states that the paper documents upgrade decisions, center-location issues in a multiple-center situation, intensity forecasting, local wind impacts, and wind-structure analysis from dropsonde and wind-profiler data.
  • It says artificial-intelligence models provided earlier alerting of the tropical depression and its movement toward southern China.
  • The same abstract also says conventional physics-based models remained useful for forecasting the wind impacts in Hong Kong, so the paper fits best as a forecast-operations piece with an explicit AI angle.
