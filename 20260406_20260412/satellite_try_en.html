Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Ensemble Forecast Sensitivity to Observations (EFSO) Applied to All-sky Infrared Radiance Observations

This paper asks how much all-sky infrared radiances matter to forecast improvement, making it an observing-system study with direct satellite relevance.

  • The abstract frames EFSO as a way to estimate each observation’s contribution to forecast improvement.
  • Here the method is applied specifically to all-sky infrared radiance observations.
  • That makes the paper highly relevant to satellite data usage and observing-system design for NWP.

Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (AGU)

Long-Range Transport Pathways of Intense Wildfire Plumes in the Northern Hemisphere Observed by the IASI/METOP Satellite Mission

Satellite missions are often the only practical way to follow hazardous plume transport across basins and continents.

  • The abstract presents a back-trajectory analysis built from satellite observations of carbon monoxide and peroxyacetyl nitrate.
  • It focuses on long-range transport of extreme wildfire plumes across the Northern Hemisphere.
  • Although not a weather-forecast paper, it is a clear satellite-observing-system study relevant to atmospheric monitoring.
