Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Radar Data Assimilation with JEDI LETKF for Ensemble Forecasting of Hurricane Ida (2021) Using a HAFS-Like Configuration

Why it matters: A clean same-week example of radar observations feeding directly into hurricane ensemble prediction.

  • The abstract evaluates direct radar assimilation inside the JEDI Local Ensemble Transform Kalman Filter framework for Hurricane Ida.
  • Radial-velocity assimilation improves inner-core wind structure, reduces track spread during cycling, and improves landfall-location forecasts.
  • Reflectivity assimilation contributes more to rainfall skill, especially for extreme rainfall and outer-rainband precipitation before landfall.

Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Examining the Impact of Different Uncrewed Aerial System (UAS) Sampling Strategies on Fog Forecasts in the Ohio Valley Using Observing System Simulation Experiments (OSSEs)

Why it matters: This adds a same-week Weather and Forecasting article that is genuinely forecast-oriented rather than a loose operations note.

  • The abstract uses observing system simulation experiments for three impactful Ohio Valley fog events to compare two UAS sampling strategies.
  • Both sampling strategies improve fog forecast skill after assimilation, showing that denser nocturnal boundary-layer observations can matter.
  • Profiling-style UAS networks improve temperature and dewpoint analyses aloft more strongly, which the authors tie to broader aviation-hazard forecasting value.

Journal of Geophysical Research: Atmospheres (AGU)

Subseasonal Forecasting of Snow Cover and Cold Compound Extremes: Insights From MPAS-A Over Midlatitude East Asia

Why it matters: A strong NWP pick for the week because the abstract is explicit about lead-time skill, error sources, and model-physics sensitivity.

  • The abstract says MPAS-A can forecast rapid snow-cover expansion and temperature drops up to two pentads ahead, with detectable skill at three pentads.
  • Beyond four pentads the forecasts become unreliable, so the paper gives a clear upper bound on useful subseasonal skill in this setup.
  • Biases come mainly from underestimated snowfall, and the results are sensitive to the land-surface snow-cover-fraction scheme.
