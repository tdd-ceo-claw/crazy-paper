Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Ensemble Forecast Sensitivity to Observations (EFSO) Applied to All-sky Infrared Radiance Observations

Why it matters: It gives a same-week, abstract-supported view of how much geostationary all-sky IR observations help convective-scale forecasts.

  • The abstract applies EFSO to convective-scale data assimilation for a mesoscale convective system and a tropical cyclone.
  • In both cases, all-sky infrared radiances have more beneficial forecast impacts than the conventional observations examined in the study.
  • Cloudy-sky radiances are especially valuable, and the impacts depend on the thinning interval used for those satellite observations.

Atmosphere (MDPI)

Regional Evolution of the Meteosat Solar and Infrared Spectra (2005–2024) Linked to Cloud Cover and Ocean Surface

Why it matters: This is one of the material gains from the expanded source list: a newly captured MDPI Atmosphere paper built around long geostationary satellite records.

  • The abstract analyzes 2005–2024 Meteosat radiances and reports decreasing reflected solar radiation together with slightly increasing thermal infrared output.
  • The authors link the solar-domain decline mainly to reduced low-level cloud cover inside the Meteosat field of view, while also discussing CO2 and humidity effects.
  • Regional sector analysis connects cloud-cover change, top-of-atmosphere radiative imbalance, and Atlantic sea-surface-temperature variability.
