Monthly Weather Review (AMS)

Primary: Radar

Characteristics of Layers of Enhanced Spectrum Width within Northeast United States Winter Precipitation Events

A Ka-band radar climatology shows where enhanced spectrum-width layers appear inside winter precipitation and why they matter for diagnosing shear-rich storm structure.

  • Crossref abstract metadata says KASPR observations from Long Island were used to build a first winter-event climatology of enhanced spectrum-width layers from 2017 to 2021.
  • The abstract attributes the layers mainly to resolved shear signatures, with turbulence playing a more conditional role.
  • That makes the paper a strong radar-interpretation study rather than a broader NWP or satellite article.

Ensemble of Data Assimilation (EDA) Impact Studies in Support of NOAA’s Next-Generation Microwave-Sounding Missions

Future microwave sounders are evaluated through ensemble data-assimilation experiments to quantify their forecast value inside an operational prediction system.

  • The Crossref abstract says the study uses the ECMWF Integrated Forecasting System and the ensemble-of-data-assimilation method to assess future NOAA NEON microwave-sounding concepts.
  • It finds meaningful short-range forecast-uncertainty reductions from an ATMS-like early-morning orbit, with additional but more modest gains from new SMBA channels.
  • Primary placement is NWP because the core evidence is forecast-impact assessment, while Satellite remains a secondary label because the paper is still about microwave observing-system design.

Journal of Hydrometeorology (AMS)

Primary: SatelliteSecondary: AI

Deep Learning for Multisatellite Precipitation Retrievals: Impact of Tomorrow.io’s Microwave Sounders

This study uses deep learning to turn multisatellite imagery and microwave sounders into near-real-time global precipitation estimates.

  • The abstract states that the retrieval combines geostationary imagers, polar-orbiting microwave sensors, and Tomorrow.io microwave sounders to estimate near-surface precipitation every 10 minutes at about 4-km resolution.
  • A convolutional neural network handles sparse microwave swaths and improves precipitation skill by 5%–10% over the previous version, with larger gains versus public near-real-time products.
  • Primary placement goes to Satellite because the scientific target is precipitation retrieval from spaceborne sensors, while AI is shown as a visible secondary method label.

Weather and Forecasting (AMS)

Primary: Radar

Meteorological Benefits and Operational Applications of S-Band, Rapid-Scanning, Dual-Polarization Radar for Hail-Producing Storms

Rapid-scanning dual-polarization radar resolves hailstorm evolution that slower operational update cycles can miss.

  • The abstract says conventional volumetric update times are often too slow for some rapidly evolving hailstorm processes.
  • Using NSSL's research WSR-88D radar KOUN, the study tracks polarimetric hail signatures, hail descent behavior, and updraft changes through the storm life cycle.
  • Its emphasis is squarely operational radar meteorology, especially warning support for hail-producing storms.

Journal of Atmospheric and Oceanic Technology (AMS)

Primary: Radar

Improving NEXRAD Velocity Retrievals Using Multi-PRT Scans with Regression Processing

The paper tackles the classic range–velocity ambiguity problem in NEXRAD and shows how combined recovery methods improve usable Doppler velocity coverage.

  • Crossref abstract text identifies range–velocity ambiguity and low-elevation velocity voids as a core pulsed-Doppler radar data-quality problem.
  • It combines VRAD and SZ(8/64) regression processing for stronger recovery of ambiguous or missing Doppler velocities.
  • Because the contribution is about radar signal processing and surveillance quality, radar is the clear primary bucket.

Primary: AISecondary: NWP

The Primacy of Physical Simulation in the Age of AI: A Critique of ML for Weather Forecasting

A concise perspective piece argues that machine-learning estimation should not replace physics-based weather simulation.

  • The accessible abstract is brief but explicit: machine-learning statistical estimation neither can nor should be expected to supplant physics-based simulation for weather forecasting.
  • Its scientific conversation is about AI forecasting claims rather than a new observing system or standalone dynamical-model experiment.
  • That makes AI the best primary category, with NWP shown second because the critique is aimed directly at weather-forecasting practice.
